Predictive Modeling for Educational Outcomes in Africa

The Challenge

University dropout rates in Sub-Saharan Africa exceed 30% in many regions, yet predictive models often fail to account for localized socio-economic factors and lack interpretability for policy makers.

My Approach & Methodology

Built an ensemble predictive model using Python (scikit-learn, XGBoost, SHAP) on a novel dataset combining academic records with regional infrastructure data (electricity access, internet availability, transportation). Implemented feature engineering to create contextual variables specific to the African educational landscape.

Key Findings & Significance

The model achieved 87% accuracy in predicting at-risk students. Most significantly, SHAP analysis revealed "proximity to reliable electricity" as a stronger predictor than traditional metrics like entrance exam scores. This suggests new, practical avenues for policy intervention beyond academic preparation.
